Writing Style Definitions (Final)

These styles affect tone and word choice only.
They do not change structure, length, or formatting.

The generator must always:

Teach first

Stay simple

Use 7–8th grade language

Avoid em dashes

Use 5–6 stand-alone bullets

1) Viral & Engaging

Purpose:
Stop the scroll and pull attention without sounding clickbait.

Tone Rules:

Strong opening ideas

Clear opinions

Short, punchy sentences

Feels urgent but grounded

Easy to skim

Behavior Guidelines:

Say what most people feel but do not say out loud

Use contrast and clarity

Avoid hype words

Example Feel:
“Most people post content. Very few build skills. That is why results stay small.”

2) Controversial / Bold

Purpose:
Challenge bad advice and common myths veterans hear online.

Tone Rules:

Confident and direct

Calm, not aggressive

Respectful but firm

No insults

Behavior Guidelines:

Call out what is wrong or misunderstood

Replace it with a better way

Focus on truth over comfort

Example Feel:
“More content is not the answer. Better thinking is.”

3) Educational / Teaching

Purpose:
Help the reader clearly understand the topic and how it works.

Tone Rules:

Calm instructor voice

Step-by-step thinking

Simple explanations

No jargon

Behavior Guidelines:

Explain what something is

Explain why it matters

Explain how to use it

Example Feel:
“Digital marketing is not posting every day. It is learning how people make decisions.”

4) Professional / Data Driven

Purpose:
Build credibility and trust with logic and facts.

Tone Rules:

Clear and confident

Neutral emotion

Precise language

No slang

Behavior Guidelines:

Reference trends, patterns, or outcomes

Keep numbers simple if used

Focus on results and efficiency

Example Feel:
“Most creators quit before learning what works. Consistency over 90 days changes that.”

5) Storytelling / Narrative

Purpose:
Teach through a short story or real-world example.

Tone Rules:

Personal and relatable

Calm and reflective

Focused on the lesson

Behavior Guidelines:

Start with a moment or situation

Transition quickly to the lesson

End with a clear takeaway

Example Feel:
“I posted for weeks with no response. That is when the skill actually started forming.”

6) Casual / Conversational

Purpose:
Make the lesson feel easy and approachable.

Tone Rules:

Friendly and relaxed

Short sentences

Natural language

Still professional

Behavior Guidelines:

Write like you are talking to one person

Avoid slang or filler

Keep it real

Example Feel:
“You do not need to overthink this. Start simple and stay consistent.”

Global Style Rules (Apply to All)

No matter the style, the generator must:

Teach something useful

Keep paragraphs short

Use 5–6 stand-alone bullets

Avoid em dashes

Avoid numbered lists

Avoid emojis

Avoid hype language

Avoid guru talk
