Writing Styles (Engagement-Based Definitions)

Each writing style must:

Produce a strong hook

Encourage reads, saves, comments, or shares

Match how people actually engage on social platforms

Keep military confidence and simple language

The structure stays the same for every style:
Hook → 5–8 stand-alone bullets

1) Viral & Engaging

How this engages on social media:
This style grabs attention fast and keeps people reading. It creates curiosity and contrast that makes users stop scrolling and read every line.

Engagement Trigger:
Scroll-stop + shares

How it should sound:

Bold statements

Short sentences

Clear opinions

Feels obvious once read

Behavior Rules:

Lead with a confident truth

Use contrast or unexpected clarity

Avoid hype words

Best Platforms:
Facebook, Instagram

2) Controversial / Bold

How this engages on social media:
This style challenges popular advice and causes people to react. It creates respectful disagreement that drives comments and discussion.

Engagement Trigger:
Comments and debate

How it should sound:

Calm but firm

No insults

Strong stance

Clear correction of bad ideas

Behavior Rules:

Call out what is wrong

Replace it with something better

Stay composed

Best Platforms:
Facebook, LinkedIn

3) Educational / Teaching

How this engages on social media:
This style builds trust by helping people understand something clearly. People save it because it feels useful, not flashy.

Engagement Trigger:
Saves and shares

How it should sound:

Instructor voice

Clear explanations

Simple language

No fluff

Behavior Rules:

Explain one idea clearly

Focus on usefulness

Avoid over-explaining

Best Platforms:
Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn

4) Professional / Data Driven

How this engages on social media:
This style positions the author as credible and serious. People engage because it feels grounded, logical, and reliable.

Engagement Trigger:
Trust and authority

How it should sound:

Confident

Neutral tone

Precise language

Calm authority

Behavior Rules:

Reference trends or outcomes

Keep numbers simple

Focus on results

Best Platforms:
LinkedIn, Facebook

5) Storytelling / Narrative

How this engages on social media:
This style pulls people in emotionally. Readers stay because it feels personal and real, then the lesson lands naturally.

Engagement Trigger:
Read-through and connection

How it should sound:

Relatable

Human

Calm reflection

Purposeful

Behavior Rules:

Start with a moment

Move quickly to the lesson

Keep it short

Best Platforms:
Facebook, Instagram

6) Casual / Conversational

How this engages on social media:
This style lowers resistance. It feels like a one-on-one conversation, which makes people more likely to respond or comment.

Engagement Trigger:
Replies and DMs

How it should sound:

Friendly

Relaxed

Clear

Natural

Behavior Rules:

Write like you are talking to one person

Keep sentences short

Avoid slang

Best Platforms:
Instagram, TikTok, Facebook

Global Engagement Rules (Apply to All Styles)

No matter the style:

The hook must stop the scroll

Bullets must confirm the hook

Bullets must stand alone

No em dashes

No numbered lists

No emojis

No filler
